How it works?

The system is purpose-built for the selection of calibrated plates in the context of Streetlifting, offering a solution to the intricate task of plate selection with precision and efficiency. The process involves several key steps:
  • Plate Selection as a Mathematical Problem: We view the challenge of choosing plates as a mathematical problem. This mathematical formulation closely resembles the classical knapsack problem, a well-studied optimization problem in computer science.
  • Conversion to Linear Constraint Problem: The mathematical representation of the plate selection problem is then transformed into a linear constraint problem. By doing so, we express the problem using linear equations and inequalities, which are amenable to optimization techniques commonly used in mathematical modeling.
  • Solver Utilization: To find the best solution, we employ the HiGHS mixed integer solver. This powerful tool is adept at handling problems with both integer and continuous variables. It allows us to explore various combinations of plates systematically and efficiently.
  • Dual Objective Criteria: We aim to minimize the number of plates selected and strive to minimize the difference between the total weight of plates on the left and the total weight of plates on the right, promoting symmetry and equilibrium.
  • Pyramidal Weight Arrangement: Once the optimal plate selection has been determined, our system arranges the plates in a pyramidal weight distribution. In this arrangement, the heaviest plates are positioned at the center, gradually tapering off towards the edges to ensure stability or meeting aesthetic preferences.
